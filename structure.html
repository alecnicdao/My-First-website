Ball Is Life

My favorite sport by far would be basketball. I have been playing basketball since the day I could walk. It's my passion and would anything for the game. My favorite team would be the Los Angeles Lakers only because LeBron James plays for that team. I played varsity basketball ever since the 8th grade and also AAU basketball since the age of 8 years old. What I love about the game the most that it teaches you a lot about life. One, it teaches you how to trust your players. If you can't trust your players how are you going to win. Same with many aspects in life, for example if you can't trust your wife how are you going to get through life together. Two, it teaches you how to trust yourself it different areas in the game, whether that's shooting a shot or getting put into the game in a crucial time. This relates to many different aspects in life, for example, driving for the first time, if you can't or don't trust yourself how are you going to be able to drive on the streets or in the neighborhood. Lastly, it teaches you how to control your temper at times. For example, when someone is fouling you multiple times it teaches you how to control your anger or you will either get a technical foul or maybe even get ejected out of the game. This relates to life, for example, school if you hit someone that could get you in some serious trouble, whether that get set to detention or even get suspended.

I started playing Amateur Athletic Union also known as AAU since the start of 3rd grade until about sophomore year in high school. AAU basketball is for really skilled players that are looking to play college ball in the future, but not just anyone can play AAU you have to be selected. It's relatable to like junior high sports and high school sports. The reason why I stopped playing AAU my sophomore year was to focus on my high school years in basketball. Some AAU teams I have played for would be Metro, DASH, Farmington, and Bingham. And the schools I played for are North Layton Junior High, and Northridge High. I had a great experience playing ball for many many years, but after my senior year I got really sick to the point where I gave up playing ball for little. Which I mean was a good wake up call to myself to think about my career and what I want to do with it, which was when I started to really enjoy coding and just the different aspects in a computer.

I started to build an interest in computers going into my 2nd semester here at Weber State Univerisity (Spring 2016). My major at first was Communications, the reason why I changed it was first I saw that you can't really make much with a Communications degree unless you get a Masters in it or higher, which for me I don't want to go to school for that long. At the same time I wasn't really passionate about the major. So I had a talk with my parents and basically we talked about my interests and that lead to me majoring in Computer Science and ever since my 2nd semester here at Weber State I have been a CS major and so far I am loving it.
